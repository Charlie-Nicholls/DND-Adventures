### Plan
> [!boxed|no-t]
> **Solar Protector**
> People were staring at the man on the bench. It was not entirely obvious why, not because he was a strange thing to stare at, but because it was not clear which reason they chose. It might have been the fact he was shifting and fidgeting awkwardly in the artificial gravity. Real gravity is imperfect, it changes with the density of the rocks below you, with your altitude, and though few can consciously tell the difference, there is something in your brain reacts strangely to the smoothness of artificial gravity. The man on the bench has spent the past few years fighting on the surface of planets, his brain hasn't adjusted and is still telling him that something is very wrong. This isn't helped by the fact the man on the bench is in full paladin plate armour and helmet - despite the fact the nearest skirmish is over a dozen light years away and his movements are causing the metal to clang loudly. Probably the main reason they're staring though is the fact that the man on the bench is sat directly underneath a poster of himself in which he is posing heroically and instructing the viewer join the army and do their part for the war. It is an unusual place to put it, given that, by the locations very nature, everyone who could see it had already done so.
